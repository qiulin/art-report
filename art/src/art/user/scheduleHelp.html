Notes:
Field   Allowed Values   Allowed Special Characters
Minute   0-59   , - * /
Hour   0-23   , - * /
Day (Day of the month)   1-31   , - * ? / L W
Month   1-12 or JAN-DEC   , - * /
Week Day (Day of the week)   1-7 or SUN-SAT   , - * ? / L #

The '*' character is used to specify all values. For example, "*" in the minute field means "every minute".

The '-' character is used to specify ranges. For example, "10-12" in the hour field means "the hours 10, 11 and 12".

The ',' character is used to specify additional values. For example, "MON,WED,FRI" in the week day field means "the days Monday, Wednesday, and Friday".

The '/' character is used to specify increments. For example, "0/15" in the minute field means "the minute 0, and every 15 minutes afterwards" i.e. the minutes 0, 15, 30, and 45.

The '?' character is allowed for the day of the month and week day fields. It is used to specify "no specific value". It is not possible to define specific values for both the day of the month and the week day fields. If you specify a value in the day of the month field, set the week day field to "?". If you specify a value in the week day field, set the day of the month to "?".

The 'L' character is allowed for the day of the month and week day fields. It is short-hand for "last". For example, "L" in the day of the month field means "the last day of the month" - day 31 for January, day 28 for February on non-leap years etc. The value "L" in week day field is equivalent to "7" or "SAT" i.e Saturday. If used in the week day field after another value, it means "the last certain weekday of the month" - for example "6L" means "the last Friday of the month". When using the 'L' option, do not specify additional lists of values in the field.

The 'W' character is allowed for the day of the month field. It is used to specify the weekday (Monday-Friday) nearest the given day. For example, "15W" in the day of the month field means "the nearest weekday to the 15th of the month". So if the 15th is a Saturday, the job will run on Friday the 14th. If the 15th is a Sunday, the job will run on Monday the 16th. If the 15th is a Tuesday, then it will run on Tuesday the 15th. If "1W" is specified as the day of the month and the 1st is a Saturday, the job will run on Monday the 3rd. It will not cross the month boundary. If the 'W' character is used, there can only be one value in the field. No additional lists or ranges are allowed.

The '#' character is allowed for the week day field. It is used to specify "the nth certain weekday day of the month". For example, the value "6#3" in the week day field means "the third Friday of the month" (day 6 = Friday and "#3" = the 3rd one in the month). Another example: "2#1" means "the first Monday of the month". If the '#' character is used, there can only be one value in the field. No additional values are allowed e.g. "3#1,6#3" is not allowed.

The legal characters and the names of months and days of the week are not case sensitive.
